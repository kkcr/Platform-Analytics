What is Platform Analytics?

Centralize performance analytics, user experience analytics, and reporting in one location. Share actionable insights quickly and easily.
  • Anticipate trends, prioritize resources, and continuously improve with real-time analytics.
  • Create on-demand reports using a single data model for instant, up-to-date insights.
  • Uncover trends and patterns using in-platform process mining.

    • Examples of Analytics

    Platform Analytics ships with pre-configured Dashboards and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) tailored to support a variety of processes. Below are some examples of Dashboards targeting different process stakeholders.
  • The Incident Management Dashboard provides analytics on incident volume, state, category, priority, resolution rates, etc., and organizes the information by incident State.
  • The Virtual Agent Overview Dashboard provides analytics on conversations, their completion status and duration, topics, channels, and users.
  • The Vulnerability Analytics Dashboard provides an executive view into vulnerabilities and vulnerable items, helping the Vulnerability Admin pinpoint areas of concern quickly.
  • The HR Executive Dashboard provides accountability, progress, performance metrics, and status of HR cases.

    • What is Really Needed?

  • Metrics clearly connected to goals that matter to the Glide Haven leadership and stakeholders.
  • Dashboards that provide actionable information.
  • Analytics within the context of a process or a workflow supporting daily actions and behavior.
  • Dashboards and visualizations designed to tell a clear story, tailored for the needs of the audience.

    • Key Insight

    Visualize actionable KPIs that tell the story of your business process and display the right level of information to their intended viewers.

    Table Queries:

  • Return a data summary measured at a specific time. It describes a process at the current moment.
  • Measure process outputs when no historical Trend is required.
  • Provide real-time status updates and summaries of past information.

    • Analytics Trend:

  • Presents a series of measurements collected over time. The Trend describes how a process has changed and evolved over a period of time.
  • Visualizes performance Trends against targets and uses notifications. This allows a Process Owner to take action as soon as performance degradation is detected.
  • Provides the ability to correlate multiple business processes and derive new metrics.
  • Example: What is the percentage of Critical incidents not worked on in the last 24 hours?

    Key Platform Analytics Objects

    1. Indicator (KPI)

      Defines a specific measurement you can count or calculate to assess process performance and forecast future trends. Example: The Number of open incidents indicator returns a count of all open incidents.

    2. Breakdown

      The ability to group (breakdown) indicator scores by a qualitative attribute, such as Priority, Category, or Assignment Group. Example: The Priority breakdown applied to the Number of open incidents indicator shows performance across all incident prioritiesâ€”Planning, Low, Moderate, High, and Critical.

    3. Data Collector

      The engine that takes periodic snapshots of your process tables and stores them in the Scores and Snapshots tables for performance analysis. Example: The [PA Incident] Daily Data Collection job runs daily and populates data for multiple indicators, retrieving the right set of records from the incident table.

    4. KPI Details

      An exploratory view of indicator results used for detailed analysis. It lets you see trends, predictions, breakdowns, and the associated records for a specific indicator. Example: The % of incidents resolved by first assigned group KPI details allow you to scrutinize all indicator data collected so far.

    5. Data Visualization

      A reusable view of indicator scores that can present data as a single value, a historical trend chart, or one of many other visualizations. These can be arranged on the dashboard canvas. Example: A visualization displays Number of open incidents as a Time Series trend.

    6. Next Experience Dashboard

      A collection of visualizations that describe a process or service behavior and target a specific audience. Example: The Incident Management Dashboard contains Incident-related visualizations (single score, breakdown, scorecard, etc.) organized in tabs to give a full picture of the Incident Management process.
